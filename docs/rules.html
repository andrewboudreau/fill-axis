Fill Axis

Rules Reference — Two-Player Territory Game

Overview

Fill Axis is a two-player territory game played on a square grid. Both players simultaneously place their pieces face-down during a placement phase. Once all pieces are placed, they are revealed and battle resolves automatically — eliminating pieces that are outnumbered by opposing attack. The player who controls the most territory at the end wins, unless one player destroys enough of the opponent's Generals to claim an early victory.

Turn Structure

  1. Players alternate turns during the Placement Phase.
  2. On your turn, drag and drop the number of pieces shown in the turn banner onto any empty cell.
  3. Pieces are placed face-down — your opponent cannot see your piece types until reveal.
  4. Once you have placed all your required tiles for the turn, the turn ends automatically (local mode) or you press End Turn → (multiplayer).
  5. When both players have placed all their pieces, the Reveal Phase begins.
Async mode: In turn-based games, each player's turn is stored to Azure Blob Storage after placement. The opponent polls for updates when they open the game next — no need to be online simultaneously.

Fog of War

During the Placement Phase, each player's pieces appear as ? tokens to the opponent. You can see your own pieces but not your opponent's piece types.

  • In local (same screen) mode, the current player sees their own pieces; the other player's pieces show as ?.
  • In online multiplayer, each player always sees their own pieces and the opponent's pieces as hidden ? tokens until reveal.
  • Attack zone shading is not shown during the Placement Phase, so you cannot deduce piece types from coverage alone.

Battle Resolution

After all pieces are placed, battle resolves automatically in rounds:

  1. Build Attack Map — For every living piece, calculate all cells it attacks. Each cell gets a counter per team: how many pieces from that team attack it.
  2. Check Elimination — A piece is eliminated if the opponent's attack count on its cell is strictly greater than its own team's attack count on that cell.
  3. Apply Deaths — All pieces marked for elimination this round are removed simultaneously.
  4. Repeat — Rebuild the attack map without dead pieces and check again. Continue until no new eliminations occur.
  5. Score Territory — After resolution, each cell is scored: the team with a higher attack count on that cell owns it. Contested cells (equal non-zero) count for neither.
Elimination is simultaneous within a round. A piece that would be eliminated cannot "save itself" by also eliminating its attacker in the same round.

Win Conditions

There are two ways to win:

1. Win by General

Destroy enough of the opponent's Generals. The threshold depends on board size:

BoardGenerals per playerWin condition
4×41Destroy the opponent's only General
6×62Have 2+ more surviving Generals than opponent, or opponent has 0
8×84Have 2+ more surviving Generals than opponent, or opponent has 0

2. Win by Territory

If neither player achieves a General victory, the player who exclusively controls more cells wins. Contested cells (attacked equally by both sides) count for neither player. If territory is exactly equal, the game ends in a Draw.

Fair Play & Cryptography

Fill Axis uses a cryptographic commitment scheme to ensure neither player can change their piece placement after seeing the opponent's board:

  • Before reveal, each player's board is hashed with a secret salt: SHA-256(board + salt). Only the hash is shared — the board stays hidden.
  • The salt is kept secret until after the opponent has also committed. Once both hashes are exchanged, neither player can alter their placement without detection.
  • At reveal, both players send their board and salt. The opponent verifies the hash matches the earlier commitment — any tampering is immediately caught.
After the game ends, both the commitment hash and the salt are saved to the audit record. The secret is already revealed at this point, so including the salt allows anyone to re-verify the game result. See 🔐 Crypto Demo for an interactive walkthrough.

Device Identity & Privacy

Fill Axis uses a lightweight, server-free identity system:

  • One device = one player identity. On first visit, a random 32-byte ID and an ECDSA keypair are auto-generated in your browser.
  • Your identity is persisted in localStorage. Clearing your browser storage permanently loses your identity — this is by design, keeping you in full control.
  • Share your device ID with opponents so they can recognize you across games.
  • No account, no server, no login. Your keys never leave your device.

Audit & Verification

Every completed game produces a verifiable audit record:

  • After every P2P or async game, an audit record is saved to blob storage automatically.
  • The record contains: both commitment hashes, both salts, all piece positions, and the winner.
  • Anyone with the room code can load and inspect it at 📋 audit.html.
  • The salt is always included in the audit record — the game is over, so the secret has already been revealed and there is nothing left to hide.
